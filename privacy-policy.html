Privacy Policy

Last Updated: February 11, 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how CompoundCalc.org ("we," "us," or "our") collects, uses, and shares information when you visit our website at https://compoundcalc.org (the "Site"). By using the Site, you agree to the collection and use of information in accordance with this policy.

Information We Collect

Information You Provide

CompoundCalc.org is a free compound interest calculator tool. We do not require you to create an account, provide your name, or submit any personal information to use our calculator. If you contact us via email, we will collect the information you provide in your message, such as your name and email address.

Information Collected Automatically

When you visit our Site, certain information is collected automatically through cookies and similar technologies. This may include:

  • Your IP address
  • Browser type and version
  • Operating system
  • Referring website or URL
  • Pages visited on our Site and time spent on each page
  • Date and time of your visit
  • Device information (screen resolution, device type)

Cookies and Tracking Technologies

Our Site uses cookies and similar tracking technologies to improve your experience and to serve relevant advertisements. Cookies are small text files stored on your device by your web browser.

Types of Cookies We Use

  • Essential Cookies: These are necessary for the Site to function properly. They enable basic features like page navigation.
  • Analytics Cookies: These help us understand how visitors interact with our Site by collecting information about pages visited, time on site, and navigation paths. This data is aggregated and anonymous.
  • Advertising Cookies: These are used by our advertising partners, including Google AdSense, to serve ads relevant to your interests. These cookies may track your browsing activity across multiple websites.

Google AdSense and Third-Party Advertising

We use Google AdSense to display advertisements on our Site. Google AdSense uses cookies, including the DoubleClick cookie, to serve ads based on your prior visits to our Site and other websites on the internet.

Google's use of advertising cookies enables it and its partners to serve ads to you based on your visit to our Site and other sites on the internet. You may opt out of personalized advertising by visiting Google Ads Settings. Alternatively, you can opt out of third-party vendor cookies for personalized advertising by visiting www.aboutads.info/choices.

Third-party vendors, including Google, use cookies to serve ads based on your prior visits to our website or other websites. You can review how Google manages data in its ads products by visiting Google's Privacy & Terms page.

How We Use Your Information

We use the information collected for the following purposes:

  • To provide and maintain our Site and calculator tools
  • To understand how visitors use our Site so we can improve it
  • To serve relevant advertisements through Google AdSense
  • To respond to your inquiries if you contact us
  • To detect and prevent technical issues or abuse

Data Sharing

We do not sell your personal information. We may share information with the following parties:

  • Google AdSense: As described above, Google collects data through cookies to serve personalized advertisements.
  • Analytics Providers: We may use analytics services to understand Site usage patterns. These providers process data on our behalf.
  • Legal Requirements: We may disclose information if required by law, regulation, or legal process.

Your Rights Under GDPR (European Users)

If you are located in the European Economic Area (EEA), you have certain data protection rights under the General Data Protection Regulation (GDPR). These include:

  • Right of Access: You have the right to request copies of the personal data we hold about you.
  • Right to Rectification: You have the right to request that we correct any information you believe is inaccurate or incomplete.
  • Right to Erasure: You have the right to request that we erase your personal data under certain conditions.
  • Right to Restrict Processing: You have the right to request that we restrict the processing of your personal data under certain conditions.
  • Right to Data Portability: You have the right to request that we transfer data we have collected to another organization or directly to you.
  • Right to Object: You have the right to object to our processing of your personal data under certain conditions.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at contact@compoundcalc.org. We will respond to your request within 30 days.

Your Rights Under CCPA (California Users)

If you are a California resident, the California Consumer Privacy Act (CCPA) provides you with specific rights regarding your personal information:

  • Right to Know: You have the right to request that we disclose what personal information we collect, use, and share about you.
  • Right to Delete: You have the right to request the deletion of personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to Opt-Out: You have the right to opt out of the sale of your personal information. We do not sell personal information, but third-party cookies used for advertising may constitute a "sale" under CCPA. You can opt out by adjusting your cookie preferences or using browser-based opt-out tools.
  • Right to Non-Discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising any of your CCPA rights.

To exercise your CCPA rights, contact us at contact@compoundcalc.org.

Cookie data is retained according to the expiration periods set by the respective cookie providers. You can clear cookies at any time through your browser settings.
