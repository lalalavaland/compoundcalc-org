Why Compound Interest Matters

Compound interest is often called the "eighth wonder of the world" because of its extraordinary ability to grow wealth over time. Unlike simple interest, which only earns returns on your initial deposit, compound interest earns returns on both your principal and your accumulated interest. This creates an exponential growth curve that becomes more dramatic the longer you stay invested.

Consider this: a one-time investment of $10,000 at 7% annual return grows to $76,123 after 30 years with compound interest. With simple interest, that same investment would only reach $31,000. The difference of $45,123 was generated entirely by the compounding effect, with no additional contributions required.

This is why starting early matters so much. Every additional year of compounding amplifies your returns. The earlier you understand these principles, the more time you have to put them to work for your financial future.
